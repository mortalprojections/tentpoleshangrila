#007

the ticking of the timepiece at my side was an ever-present companion, its rhythm a constant reminder of the impermanence of the present and the need to seek a new path forward. like a seductive siren..., its melody lulled me into a state of limbo trapped between the known and the unknown, unable to fully embrace either. despite the said melodies, i resisted its pull.

the shackles of time, which i had imposed upon myself, became a symbol of my unwillingness to accept reality as it was. instead, i chose to create my own, one that was darker and more foreboding, painted in the hues of my own insecurities and fears. as i lay trapped in my own mental prison, i realized the truth: the clock was a burden of my own making, a reminder of the fleeting nature of existence and the urgency to seize the moment.

the weight of sanity, like a bitter pill i could not swallow, had taken on the form of a leash, binding me to the chains of time and preventing me from truly living in the present. yet even as i chose to wallow in my own self-imposed suffering, i still yearned for a way out, for a chance to create a picture-perfect painting, unencumbered by the clutches of this mortal coil. and so the ticking of the timepiece continued to play on, a haunting reminder of the choices i had made and the opportunities i had missed. a call to awaken and step out into the light, whatever its cost.



#008

in the dead of winter, my journey began on an impulse, with no destination in mind, but with my heart set on a goal unknown. as the sound of the engine roared, and consumed... my senses, i fought back the fear of being viewed as guilty in the eyes of others. nonetheless, every sound i made, echoed my thoughts, leaving no room for doubt. words rushed out of my lips, the bitter taste of my tears only made things worse. they seemed like fallen leaves that flew away with the wind, only to return to the trees as if they had never left. my heart was too heavy with pain and fear, and the silence that followed left me hollow and empty.

it seemed like mere seconds passed before a moment of shock and confusion gripped me, one that i would not soon forget. i had too much to say, yet i was scared of saying "i" when he told me it wasn't always about me. but perhaps it was really about me. a photograph soon flashed before my eyes, depicting the innocence and joy of those who dared to dream, of those who dared to let their laughter fill the air. images came rushing out of those frozen tears, filled with regret. when the clock's arm turned, the silence seemed to crumble, and i bled out in agony, like a flower wilting in the harsh winter air. the warmth that i sought seemed increasingly distant, and the idea of building a home with another seemed like a fleeting dream.

a couple of drops were all it took for me to see how it all came crashing down. it was an impossible scenario, as if a waterfall had crumbled in the face of its old age. the blanket that once provided me solace had turned into a sheet of sharp thorns, piercing through my skin, my heart, and my very soul. the voices of those who once loved me, the voices of those who once cared, were now but a distant memory. piercing voices, silenced whimpers, police sirens, never-ending arguments concerning the currency of all those sinful.

little did i know it would make me into a withering flower who never had the chance to bloom as ‘twas hidden from the sun, lacking almost all of its needs. i used to quietly scream for help, such a contrasting habit. and yet today, i felt like all those voices that i reserved for when i needed it left me alone again. in the end, I realized that it wasn’t for me. i should’t have asked for it. ‘twasn’t always about me. not ‘I’, ‘me’, nor ‘myself’. ‘twas just all in my head after all.
